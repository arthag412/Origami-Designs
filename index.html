Facts about Origami

It wasn’t always called Origami. For the initial 1500 years or so of its existence, origami was actually known as orikata, a Japanese word that translates to “Folded Paper”. Origami was first only practiced by the Elite. Paper was originally created sometime around 100 A.D. ... It is a major part of Japanese Wedding Rituals. Speaking of surprising uses, one very important function origami plays is in Japanese Wedding Rituals.

Some facts about Camel

  1. They can go for weeks without drinking water.
  2. They store fat in parts of their bodies known as humps.
  3. They can live for about 40 years.
  4. Camel milk is among the healthiest milk in the world.
  5. Camels can sit comfortably on the hot sand.



Some facts about Chameleon

  1. Chameleons have impressively long tongues.
  2. Chameleons don’t tend to live too long.
  3. Chameleons have varied diets.
  4. Chameleons will differ hugely in terms of size.
  5. Chameleons are able to spy and spot potential prey from up to 10m.



Some facts about Pigeon

  1. They can fly very fast.
  2. They stay very neat and clean.
  3. They are considered spiritual by some religions.
  4. They once beacme extinct.
  5. Their sizes vary a lot.



Some facts about Bear

  1. A bear’s normal heartbeat is 40 beats per minute.
  2. Only the polar bear is a true carnivore. All other bears are omnivores.
  3. Bears can run up to 40 miles per hour.
  4. Bears live as long as 30 years .
  5. Bears are very smart.



Some facts about Panda

  1. They do not hibernate.
  2. Baby pandas are born pink.
  3. Giant pandas grow to between 1.2m and 1.5m, and weigh between 75kg and 135kgs.
  4. The giant panda’s scientific name is Ailuropoda melanoleuca, which means “black and white cat-foot”.
  5. These magnificent mammals are omnivores.



Some facts about Cicada

  1. Cicada species vary in size, between 0.75 of an inch and 2.25 inches.
  2. A cicada’s eye color can be blue, red or white.
  3. Cicadas are known for the loud sounds they can produce.
  4. Periodic cicadas are only found in the United States of America.
  5. There are about 3,000 known species of cicadas worldwide.
